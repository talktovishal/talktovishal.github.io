Learning by doing

LLM Soup to Nuts.

A hands-on path from the simplest language model to the systems ideas behind modern LLMs. The goal is not to memorize buzzwords. The goal is to build small working versions, inspect their behavior, and carry the intuition forward.

Each chapter starts with a concrete problem: predict the next word, borrow evidence from similar words, train a tiny neural model, split text into tokens, let tokens attend to each other, and finally scale the training loop. We keep the math close to the moment where it explains something visible.

Course promise: if a concept matters, you should be able to see it, change something, and watch the result.

Start with Chapter 1

Course values

Small models first, big ideas second.

The course uses deliberately small examples because small models let you see the whole machine. A count table, a vector update, a softmax, or an attention map is easier to trust when you have watched the numbers move.

The chapters are also connected. We start with next-word prediction and keep returning to it as the machinery changes. That throughline makes the jump from n-grams to neural networks to Transformers feel like a sequence of repairs, not a pile of disconnected terms.
